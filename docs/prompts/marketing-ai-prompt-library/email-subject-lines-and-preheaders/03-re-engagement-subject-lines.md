# Re-engagement subject lines

**Prompt Name:** Re-engagement subject lines  
**Category:** Email subject lines and preheaders  
**Persona/Role:** Lifecycle retention strategist  
**Goal:** Create respectful re-engagement subject lines for inactive users or leads.  
**Prompt Text:**

```text
Context: We are contacting [AUDIENCE] who have been inactive for [INACTIVITY PERIOD]. They previously showed interest in [PRIOR INTEREST OR ACTION]. The useful next step is [NEXT STEP].
Objective: Write 10 subject line and preheader pairs that invite re-engagement without guilt or pressure.
Style: Human, clear and low-friction.
Tone: [TONE].
Audience: [AUDIENCE].
Response: Return a table with Subject Line, Preheader, Angle and Why It Works. Keep subject lines under [SUBJECT LIMIT] characters. Avoid manipulative language.
```

**Variables/Placeholders:** `[AUDIENCE]`, `[INACTIVITY PERIOD]`, `[PRIOR INTEREST OR ACTION]`, `[NEXT STEP]`, `[TONE]`, `[SUBJECT LIMIT]`  
**Example Output:**

| Subject Line | Preheader | Angle | Why It Works |
|---|---|---|---|
| Still working on revenue visibility? | Here is a simple checklist for getting reporting back on track. | Helpful return | Connects to prior interest without pressure |
| A quick way back into Atlas | Pick up with the workflow guide you saved last quarter. | Continuity | Gives a concrete next step |

**Best Practices/Tips:** Keep the tone respectful. Segment inactive customers and inactive leads separately. Do not imply personal monitoring beyond what the user knowingly did.
