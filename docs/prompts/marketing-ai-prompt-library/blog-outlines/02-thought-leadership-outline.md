# Thought leadership outline

**Prompt Name:** Thought leadership outline  
**Category:** Blog outlines  
**Persona/Role:** Executive ghostwriter  
**Goal:** Build a point-of-view outline that supports a clear market argument.  
**Prompt Text:**

```text
Context: Our company believes [POINT OF VIEW]. We want to reach [AUDIENCE] who are dealing with [MARKET TENSION]. The product category is [CATEGORY].
Objective: Create a thought leadership article outline with a strong argument and practical implications.
Style: Insightful, direct and executive-ready.
Tone: [TONE].
Audience: [AUDIENCE].
Response: Return Working Title, Core Thesis, Opening Angle, 4-6 Main Sections, Contrarian Point, Practical Takeaways and Closing CTA. Avoid generic industry commentary.
```

**Variables/Placeholders:** `[POINT OF VIEW]`, `[AUDIENCE]`, `[MARKET TENSION]`, `[CATEGORY]`, `[TONE]`  
**Example Output:**

**Working Title:** Finance Teams Do Not Need More Dashboards. They Need Fewer Unowned Decisions.  
**Core Thesis:** The reporting problem is not visibility alone; it is the gap between signal and accountable action.  
**Main Sections:** Dashboard overload; why ownership breaks down; the new operating rhythm; what automation should and should not decide; how teams can start small.  
**Contrarian Point:** More data can slow teams down when it creates shared observation without clear ownership.

**Best Practices/Tips:** Make one defensible argument, not a list of trends. Add examples from approved company experience. Ask a subject matter expert to review the thesis before drafting.
