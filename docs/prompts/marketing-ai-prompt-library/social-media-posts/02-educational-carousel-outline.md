# Educational carousel outline

**Prompt Name:** Educational carousel outline  
**Category:** Social media posts  
**Persona/Role:** B2B content strategist  
**Goal:** Turn one educational idea into a slide-by-slide social carousel.  
**Prompt Text:**

```text
Context: We want to educate [AUDIENCE] about [TOPIC] and connect the lesson to [PRODUCT NAME] without making the post feel like a hard sell.
Objective: Create a [SLIDE COUNT]-slide carousel outline for [CHANNEL].
Style: Practical, skimmable and concrete.
Tone: [TONE].
Audience: [AUDIENCE].
Response: For each slide, provide Slide Title, Body Copy and Visual Direction. End with a soft CTA. Do not include unsupported statistics.
```

**Variables/Placeholders:** `[AUDIENCE]`, `[TOPIC]`, `[PRODUCT NAME]`, `[SLIDE COUNT]`, `[CHANNEL]`, `[TONE]`  
**Example Output:**

| Slide | Slide Title | Body Copy | Visual Direction |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Stop treating churn as a monthly surprise | Churn signals usually appear weeks before renewal. The issue is finding them early enough to act. | Split screen: late dashboard vs early signal list |
| 2 | Watch product usage changes | A sudden drop in active seats can show risk before a customer complains. | Line chart with highlighted drop |
| 3 | Make follow-up owned | Assign every risk signal to one accountable owner. | Simple owner/status board |
| 4 | Keep the workflow visible | Atlas Analytics keeps risk signals, owners and next steps in one shared view. | Product screenshot placeholder |
| 5 | Build your early-warning list | Start with your top 20 accounts this week. | Checklist graphic |

**Best Practices/Tips:** Keep each slide focused on one idea. Use visual directions that designers can execute quickly. Keep the product mention brief and tied to the lesson.
